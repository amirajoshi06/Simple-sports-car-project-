The Hudson

1932 Nash Advanced Eight four-door convertible (Series 1090). This Nash is large prestigious (luxury) car, and is classified by the Classic Car Club of America (CCCA) as a Full Classic. Power is the Nash twin-ignition 322 cubic-inch (5.27 L) overhead-valve straight-eight cylinder engine that features two sets of plugs, points, condensers, and coils operating from a single distributor. Other advanced features include synchromesh transmission, free-wheeling, automatic centralized chassis lubrication, as well as a suspension that is adjustable from inside the car. Picture taken at the Antique Automobile Club of America (AACA) 2015 Hershey meet that is held every October in Pennsylvania.
